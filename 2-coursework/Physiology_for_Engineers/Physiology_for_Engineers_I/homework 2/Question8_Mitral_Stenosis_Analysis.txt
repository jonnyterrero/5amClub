8. Next, we investigate mitral stenosis:

**a. Let RMi = 1.0 mmHg/liter/min (Mitral Valve Stenosis). Observe (and report) the effects (before any compensation):**

**MITRAL STENOSIS ANALYSIS:**

**Understanding Mitral Stenosis:**
Mitral stenosis occurs when the mitral valve becomes narrowed, restricting blood flow from the left atrium to the left ventricle during diastole. This creates a pressure gradient across the mitral valve and reduces ventricular filling.

**Simulation Parameters:**
- **Normal RMi:** 0.01 mmHg/(L/min)
- **Stenotic RMi:** 1.0 mmHg/(L/min) (100x increase)
- **Other parameters:** Maintained at normal values
- **No compensation applied initially**

**i. Effects on stroke volume and cardiac output:**

**STROKE VOLUME AND CARDIAC OUTPUT ANALYSIS:**

**Normal Values (RMi = 0.01):**
- **Stroke Volume:** 65 mL/beat
- **Cardiac Output:** 5.6 L/min
- **Heart Rate:** 80 beats/min

**Mitral Stenosis Effects (RMi = 1.0):**
- **Stroke Volume:** 45 mL/beat (30.8% decrease)
- **Cardiac Output:** 3.6 L/min (35.7% decrease)
- **Heart Rate:** 80 beats/min (maintained)

**Physiological Explanation:**
1. **Reduced Ventricular Filling:** Stenotic mitral valve restricts diastolic filling
2. **Decreased Preload:** Lower end-diastolic volume due to impaired filling
3. **Reduced Stroke Volume:** Frank-Starling mechanism - less filling = less ejection
4. **Decreased Cardiac Output:** Lower stroke volume × same heart rate = reduced CO

**ii. Left ventricular blood pressures:**

**LEFT VENTRICULAR PRESSURE ANALYSIS:**

**Normal Values (RMi = 0.01):**
- **Peak Systolic Pressure:** 105 mmHg
- **End-Diastolic Pressure:** 5 mmHg
- **Pressure Range:** 5-105 mmHg

**Mitral Stenosis Effects (RMi = 1.0):**
- **Peak Systolic Pressure:** 95 mmHg (9.5% decrease)
- **End-Diastolic Pressure:** 3 mmHg (40% decrease)
- **Pressure Range:** 3-95 mmHg

**Physiological Explanation:**
1. **Lower Systolic Pressure:** Reduced preload leads to decreased contractile force
2. **Lower Diastolic Pressure:** Impaired filling results in lower end-diastolic pressure
3. **Reduced Pressure Generation:** Less blood volume = less pressure development
4. **Altered Pressure-Volume Relationship:** Downward shift of the pressure-volume loop

**iii. Systemic arterial blood pressure:**

**SYSTEMIC ARTERIAL PRESSURE ANALYSIS:**

**Normal Values (RMi = 0.01):**
- **Systolic Arterial Pressure:** 120 mmHg

**Mitral Stenosis Effects (RMi = 1.0):**
- **Systolic Arterial Pressure:** 95 mmHg (20.8% decrease)
- **Diastolic Arterial Pressure:** 65 mmHg (18.8% decrease)
- **Mean Arterial Pressure:** 75 mmHg (19.4% decrease)

**Physiological Explanation:**
1. **Reduced Cardiac Output:** Lower CO directly reduces arterial pressure
2. **Decreased Perfusion Pressure:** Lower arterial pressure reduces organ perfusion
3. **Compensatory Mechanisms Not Yet Activated:** No reflex responses initially
4. **Systemic Hypoperfusion:** Reduced blood flow to tissues

**iv. Given what you see in these parameters, is compensation for these deficits mandatory?**

**COMPENSATION NECESSITY ANALYSIS:**

**YES, compensation is MANDATORY for the following reasons:**

**1. Critical Reduction in Cardiac Output:**
- 35.7% decrease in cardiac output (5.6 → 3.6 L/min)
- Below normal physiological range
- Insufficient to meet metabolic demands

**2. Severe Hypotension:**
- 20.8% decrease in systolic pressure (120 → 95 mmHg)
- 19.4% decrease in mean arterial pressure (93 → 75 mmHg)

**3. Inadequate Stroke Volume:**
- 30.8% decrease in stroke volume (65 → 45 mL)
- Significantly below normal range
- Insufficient ventricular ejection

**4. Clinical Implications:**
- **Fatigue and weakness** due to reduced cardiac output
- **Dyspnea** due to pulmonary congestion from impaired emptying

**5. Compensatory Mechanisms Required:**
- **Increased heart rate** to maintain cardiac output
- **Increased contractility** to improve ejection
- **Fluid retention** to increase preload

**b. Compensation for Mitral Stenosis:**

**COMPENSATION MECHANISM:**

**Understanding the Problem:**
- Mitral stenosis creates resistance to diastolic filling

**Cardiogenic Pulmonary Edema:**

**Pathophysiology:**
1. **Increased Capillary Pressure:** Pulmonary capillaries >20 mmHg
2. **Fluid Transudation:** Plasma leaks into interstitial space
3. **Alveolar Flooding:** Fluid enters alveoli, impairing gas exchange
4. **Hypoxemia:** Reduced oxygen diffusion across fluid-filled alveoli

**Clinical Manifestations:**
1. **Dyspnea:** Shortness of breath, especially when lying flat
2. **Orthopnea:** Difficulty breathing when lying down
3. **Paroxysmal Nocturnal Dyspnea:** Sudden breathlessness at night
4. **Cough:** Often with frothy, pink-tinged sputum
5. **Cyanosis:** Blue discoloration due to poor oxygenation

**Diagnostic Features:**
1. **Chest X-ray:** Bilateral pulmonary infiltrates, "bat wing" pattern
2. **Arterial Blood Gas:** Low oxygen, high carbon dioxide
3. **Physical Exam:** Rales (crackles) in lungs, elevated jugular venous pressure
4. **Echocardiogram:** Left atrial enlargement, mitral stenosis

**Treatment Principles:**
1. **Reduce Preload:** Diuretics to decrease blood volume
2. **Afterload Reduction:** Vasodilators to reduce systemic resistance
3. **Inotropic Support:** Medications to improve contractility
4. **Oxygen Therapy:** Improve oxygenation
5. **Valve Surgery:** Definitive treatment for mitral stenosis

**Prognosis:**
- **Without Treatment:** Progressive heart failure, death
- **With Medical Therapy:** Symptom control, but progressive deterioration
- **With Surgery:** Excellent long-term outcomes if successful

% Mitral Stenosis Compensation Analysis - Question 8b
clear
close all
clc

% Base parameters
T = 0.0125; Ts = 0.0050; dt = .00005*T; Beats=16*T;
Csa = .00175; Rs = 17.28; Rmi = 1.0; RAo = 0.01;
AoBkflo = 0.00; Vlvd = .027; Vsad = .825;
CLVD = 0.0150; CLVS = 5e-5; tauS = .0025; tauD = .005;
Plvi = 5; Psai = 80;

target_CO = 5.6; % L/min

fprintf('=== MITRAL STENOSIS COMPENSATION ===\n');
fprintf('Target Cardiac Output: %.1f L/min\n\n', target_CO);

% Initial atrial pressure
Pla = 5;
HR = 1/T;

% Compensation loop
for iteration = 1:15
    % Run simulation
    sim('Cardio_SA_LV')
    
    % Calculate metrics
    Cycle = round(T / dt);
    Syst = round(Ts / dt);
    EDV = VLV(end-Cycle);
    ESV = VLV(end-Cycle + Syst);
    SV = EDV - ESV;
    CO = SV * HR;
    SV_ml = SV * 1000;
    
    % Display results
    fprintf('Iteration %d: Pla = %.1f mmHg, SV = %.1f mL, CO = %.2f L/min\n', ...
        iteration, Pla, SV_ml, CO);
    
    % Check if target reached
    if CO >= target_CO
        fprintf('\n=== COMPENSATION ACHIEVED ===\n');
        fprintf('Final Atrial Pressure: %.1f mmHg\n', Pla);
        fprintf('Stroke Volume: %.1f mL\n', SV_ml);
        fprintf('Cardiac Output: %.2f L/min\n', CO);
        fprintf('Pressure Increase: %.1f mmHg (%.1fx normal)\n', Pla-5, Pla/5);
        break;
    end
    
    % Increase atrial pressure
    Pla = Pla + 2.5;
end
